# Cyber Crime #

Cyber crime is a criminal activity that involves the use of computers, mobile devices, or the internet to commit illegal acts such as stealing data, spreading viruses, online fraud, or harassment.

Real-time Examples of Cyber Crime:

  • Phishing: A person receives a fake email pretending to be from a bank, asking for login details and personal information.
  • Hacking: A hacker breaks into a company’s system and steals confidential customer data.
  • Cyberbullying: A student repeatedly sends mean or threatening messages to a classmate on social media.
  • Identity Theft: A criminal uses someone else's personal information online to apply for loans or credit cards.
  • Online Fraud: A person buys a product from a fake online shopping website and never receives the item.
  • Ransomware Attack: A virus locks all files on a user’s computer and demands money to unlock them.
  • Social Media Impersonation: Someone creates a fake social media account using another person’s name and photos to mislead others.
  • Online Stalking: An individual continuously follows and monitors someone’s activities through social media or email without consent.
  • Cyber Terrorism: Hackers attack a government or organization’s computer network to spread fear or cause harm.
  • Data Breach: A company’s customer data, including emails and passwords, is exposed and leaked on the internet.
  • Spamming: Sending large numbers of unwanted or fake promotional emails to users without permission.
  • Cyber Scams: A person receives a message saying they’ve won a lottery and need to send money to claim the prize.
  • Online Child Exploitation: Criminals use online platforms to target and exploit minors.
  • Fake Job Offers: Someone posts a fake job ad online to collect personal details from job seekers.
  • Software Piracy: Downloading and using paid software from illegal websites without purchasing a license.
