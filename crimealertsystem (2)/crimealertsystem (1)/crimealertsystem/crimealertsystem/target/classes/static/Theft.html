# Theft #

Theft is the act of taking someone else's property without permission and with the intention of keeping it permanently.

Real-time Examples of Theft:

  • Shoplifting: A person hides a bottle of perfume in their bag while shopping and leaves the store without paying for it.
  • Car Theft: Someone steals a parked car by breaking the window and driving away.
  • Pickpocketing: A thief discreetly takes a wallet from a person's pocket while they are distracted in a crowded area.
  • Burglary: A person breaks into a house through an unlocked door and steals electronics and jewelry while the owners are away.
  • Package Theft: A thief takes a package left on a doorstep after a delivery, knowing the homeowner is not home.
  • Identity Theft: Someone uses another person's personal information obtained from a data breach to open credit accounts in their name.
  • Employee Theft: A store employee takes cash from the register when no one is watching.
  • Theft from Vehicle: A thief breaks into a car and steals a laptop that was left on the seat.
  • Bicycle Theft: A person steals a bicycle that is locked up outside a store by cutting the lock.
  • Theft of Tools: A contractor leaves their tools in an unsecured area at a job site, and someone takes them without permission.
  • Credit Card Skimming: A thief installs a skimming device on an ATM to capture card information from unsuspecting users.
  • Theft of Personal Items: A student leaves their backpack unattended in a classroom, and another student takes it without asking.
  • Theft of Services: Someone uses a neighbor's Wi-Fi without permission, effectively stealing internet service.
  • Theft of Artwork: A person removes a painting from a gallery without paying for it and attempts to leave with it.
  • Theft of Gas: A driver fills up their car with gas and drives away without paying at the pump.
  • Theft of Intellectual Property: An employee copies sensitive company documents and takes them to a competitor without authorization.
